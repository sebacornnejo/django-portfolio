>_NBA Players Analysis 🠊

This comprehensive NBA Players Analysis project employs statistical and geographical methods to offer a nuanced understanding of player and team dynamics, providing valuable insights for basketball enthusiasts, analysts, and decision-makers in the sports industry.

The individual player analysis begins by examining key statistics, including player heights and their correlation with performance metrics. Michael Jordan's unique skills are also dissected. The project identifies top players and teams based on points per game and development of normalized skill statistics. Geographic analyses showcase the worldwide distribution of NBA players, emphasizing country and state-specific breakdowns.

Championship analysis delves into NBA Championship overviews, comparing season statistics with team and player performances. The age distribution of players in the top five champion teams of the last 26 years is explored, providing insights into the longevity and success of elite players. Geographic distribution is further examined by mapping NBA player distributions globally, highlighting concentrations in specific countries and U.S. states.

The identification of top players based on points per game, normalized skill statistics, and overall team performance provides a solid foundation for evaluating potential recruits and optimizing team composition. Moreover, the geographic analyses, highlighting player distributions globally and in specific regions, offer a roadmap for scouting talent hotspots and uncovering untapped reservoirs of basketball potential. This type of project serves as a powerful tool for teams aiming to make informed decisions in player recruitment, strategic planning, and the pursuit of future basketball prodigies.

>_Water Quality Prediction Project (Extended) 🠊

This Water Quality Prediction Project, an extension of the original dataset 'water_potability.csv', employs advanced machine learning techniques to develop a predictive model for classifying water quality based on the extended dataset 'water.csv'. The primary objective is to categorize water samples into different quality classes. To overcome computational constraints, the dataset is strategically sampled, resulting in a training and validation set (df) and an evaluation set (df_rest).

Various visualizations are employed to enhance understanding, including a Potability Index Bar Chart, a Distribution of Water Quality Features subplot, a Pairwise Relationship Scatter Matrix, a Correlation Heatmap, and Feature Values Distribution Box Plots. Each visualization contributes to a comprehensive exploration of the dataset.

The implementation details encompass the application of Decision Tree and K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) classifiers, both fine-tuned for optimal performance. The models' evaluation involves metrics such as accuracy, classification reports, and confusion matrices, ensuring robust performance on unseen data.

Saved models, including individual classifiers (DT and KNN) and hyperparameter-tuned models, are stored using the joblib library, allowing for seamless integration into further analysis or deployment.

This type of project can equip to stakeholders with a reliable machine learning model for predicting water quality, backed by meticulous sampling, insightful visualizations, and detailed model evaluation. The robustness of the approach positions it as a valuable tool for water quality assessment.

>_A Simple Mall Customers Segmentation 🠊

This (Simple) Mall Customers Segmentation employs a data-driven approach to understand customer behavior and derive insights that could potential targeted marketing strategies.

The dataset encompasses customer attributes, including Annual Income, Spending Score, Age, and Gender. The project unfolds through Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), employing the "elbow method" and visual exploration to discern fundamental customer groups.

The customer segmentation employs clustering algorithms (K-Means), particularly focusing on Annual Income and Spending Score, yielding valuable insights into the characteristics of each identified cluster. A gender analysis is conducted within each cluster, elucidating the distribution of customers based on gender across different segments.

This type of project offering a understanding of customer behavior. Similar analyzes can be applied to pharmaceutical studies, population analysis, urban planning, ecosystems and all types of analysis where segmentation analysis can help understand activities and patterns in limited groups.

>_ Simples Visualization with Iris Dataset 🠊

This comprehensive Python project focuses on visualizing the well-known Iris dataset using Plotly and Plotly Express, showcasing the versatility and quality of data visualizations attainable for data analysis. Organized into sections, each dedicated to specific plot types, the project covers scatterplots, boxplots, histograms, and a combined dashboard.

The scatterplot analyses delve into diverse representations, from basic scatterplots of sepal dimensions to interactive scatterplots incorporating color, size, regression lines, and marginal plots. Boxplot visualizations feature a simple presentation of sepal width and a comparative analysis employing distinct colors for different species. Histogram analyses offer insights into the distribution of sepal width, both individually and across species, culminating in a general counting histogram with a vibrant color palette.

The project culminates in a visually engaging dashboard created with Plotly's make_subplots, integrating various visualizations for a comprehensive exploration of the Iris dataset. Interactive HTML files are provided for each analysis, allowing users to explore and understand the visualizations more effectively.

As a demonstration of data visualization techniques and the power of Plotly for data analysis, this project serves as a sample of these resources when analysts and decision-makers seeking to leverage advanced visualization methods for effective data communication.

>_Simple Northwind Data Analysis (SQL+Python) 🠊

This illustrative data analysis project focuses on the Northwind database, showcasing the capabilities of Python, SQL, Pandas, and Plotly in extracting insights from business data. The analyses aim to uncover simple metrics related to product revenue, employee performance, and overall sales.

Each analysis script connects to the Northwind database, performs SQL queries, and employs Pandas and Plotly for data manipulation and visualization. This project serves as a (simple) testament to the versatility of Python and its connection with SQL databases, and the quality of graphics and visualizations achievable in data analysis.

>_Scraping through internet knowledge 🠊

This project serves as a guide for web scraping and its data visualization, providing a set of tools for extracting and visualizing information from diverse online sources. The example project, "What is Anarchy for the Internet?" showcases the application of these scripts.

Individual scripts target specific platforms, such as GoogleScraping for search results, WikiScraping for Wikipedia pages, and XScraping for X tweets. These scripts generate word clouds and bar charts, providing insightful visualizations of word frequencies. Additionally, a universal scraping_wordcloud script allows users to generate customized visualizations from input text.

Each script is thoroughly documented, acknowledging the libraries and tools that contribute to their functionality, such as the Google Custom Search JSON API, Beautiful Soup, ntscraper, WordCloud, and Plotly.
